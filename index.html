Legal Considerations for Privacy Tints

Each state has laws regulating Visible Light Transmission (VLT) percentages for window tinting. In North Carolina:

  • Front Side Windows: Minimum 35% VLT required.
  • Rear Side Windows: Any level of tint is permitted for SUVs and vans; 35% VLT for sedans.
  • Rear Windshield: Same regulations as rear side windows.

FAQs About Privacy and Security Tint

1. Can Window Tint Prevent Break-Ins?

While no tint can fully prevent break-ins, dark window film deters theft by making valuables less visible and strengthening glass.

2. Will Privacy Tint Affect Nighttime Visibility?

No, high-quality ceramic films provide privacy without making it harder to see at night.

3. Is Darker Tint Legal in North Carolina?

North Carolina requires at least 35% VLT on front side windows for sedans, while SUVs and vans can have darker tint on rear windows.
